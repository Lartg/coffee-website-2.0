Drip:
  1. Grind 20g of coffee/cup of coffee brewed
  2. Make sure your water resivior is topped off
  3. Press start.
French Press:
  1. Heat around 1 quart of water to 205 degrees
  2. Grind 60g of coffee course
  3. Add ground coffee to the bottom of your french press
  4. Fill your french press quickly to saturate all your coffee, and start a timer
  5. Pop the lid back on the french press to insulate, but do not press!
  6. After 1 minute take the lid off, and push the coffee that has floated to the top back into the water
  7. Repeat at 3 minutes
  8. At 4 minutes, press and enjoy.
Cold Brew:

Tools:

  1. Large spoon
  2. Large pot or foodsafe bucket (~2 gallons)
  3. Kitchen sieve fine enough to catch grounds
  4. Pitcher for storage

Recipe:

  1. Time: 12-16 hours, recommend brewing overnight
  2. Get your pound of coffee ground as course as possible where you bought it
  3. Put coffee into the brewing vessel, and combine with a gallon of water
  4. Stir, put a lid on, and let sit for 12-16 hours
  5. after 12 hours, taste once per hour until finished
  6. Record that time for repeatability
  7. Filter through sieve into a pitcher
  8. Refridgerate, and dilute with water when consuming
  9. Cold Brew is very strong, I recommend starting at 1 part Cold Brew : 2 Parts water
Pour Over:

Tools:

  1. Scale
  2. Gooseneck kettle
  3. Temperature control
  4. Home burr grinder
  5. Filtered funnel

Recipe:

There isn't a recipe I can recommend for a perfect pour-over coffee. With so many variables in equipment, you just have to taste a lot of coffee and know what to look for in your brew. Also the tools required for repeatable brews are more expensive than the other brew methods. I recommend that beginner brewers try one of the other methods, and if they fall in love with brewing their coffee, to invest in a nice pour-over set up.
